GyroAccelCalibration  
Updated Today (5 hours ago) by scottlib...@gmail.com

Overview

The navX MXP onboard sensors require calibration in order to yield optimal results. We highly recommend taking the time to understand this calibration process - successful calibration is vital to ensure optimal performance.

Accurate Gyroscope Calibration is crucial in order to yield valid yaw angles. Although this process occurs automatically, understanding how it works is required to obtain the best results.

If you are tempted to ignore this information, please read the section entitled "The Importance of Stillness" at the end of this page.

Gyroscope/Accelerometer Calibration

Initial Gyro Calibration

Before navX MXP units are shipped, the accelerometers and gyroscopes are initially calibrated at the factory; this calibration data is stored in flash memory and applied automatically to the accelerometer and gyroscope data each time the navX MXP is powered on.

Note that the onboard gyroscopes are sensitive to temperature changes. Therefore, since the average ambient temperature at the factory (on the island of Kauai in Hawaii) may be different than in your environment, you can optionally choose to re-calibrate the gyroscope by pressing and holding the "CAL" button for at least 10 seconds. When you release the "CAL" button, ensure that the "CAL" Led flashes briefly, and then press the "RESET" button to restart the navX MXP. When the navX MXP is re-started, it will perform the Initial Gyro Calibration - the same process that occurs at our factory. NOTE: It is very important to hold the navX MXP still, and parallel to the earth's surface, during this Initial Gyro Calibration period. You might consider performing this process before using your robot the first time it is used within a new environment (e.g., when you arrive at a FRC competition event).

The value of calibrating the gyroscope at the same temperature the navX MXP will be operated at is increased yaw accuracy. If a significant temperature shift has occurred, the yaw accuracy will be diminished until the next On-the-fly Gyro Calibration completes.

On-the-fly Gyro Calibration

In addition to Initial Gyroscope Calibration, during normal operation the navX MXP will automatically re-calibrate the gyroscope (e.g., to account for ongoing temperature changes) during operation, whenever it detects 8 seconds of no motion. This process completes after about 7-8 more seconds. Therefore each time the navX MXP is still for approximately 15 seconds, the gyroscopes are re-calibrated "on-the-fly". This re-calibration can help maintain yaw accuracy when shifts in ambient temperature occur.

In a typical FIRST FRC competition match, this means that the gyroscope/accelerometers are automatically re-calibrated at the beginning of a match, between the time when the robot is powered on and when the match actually starts.

Initial Yaw Offset Calibration

In addition to Gyroscope and Accelerometer calibration, an Initial Yaw Offset is automatically calculated each time the navX MXP starts up. The purpose of the Initial Yaw Offset is to ensure that whatever direction the "front" of the navX MXP board is pointed to at startup (after initial calibration is applied) will be considered "0 degrees".

Yaw Offset Calibration requires that the navX MXP be still for approximately 2 seconds after it starts and initial calibration is applied. After approximately 2 seconds of no motion, the navX MXP will acquire the current yaw angle, and will subtract it from future yaw measurements automatically. The navX MXP protocol includes a way to determine the yaw offset value it is currently using.

NOTE: If the navX MXP is moving during initial calibration, this Yaw Offset Calibration may take much longer than 2 seconds. Therefore it is important to keep the navX MXP still until initial calibration and Yaw Offset calibration completes.

Runtime Yaw Zeroing

Your robot software can optionally provide the robot operator a way to reset the yaw angle to Zero at any time. Please see the documentation for the navX MXP libraries for more details.

The importance of stillness

This is the most important takeaway from this discussion: It is very important that the navX MXP be held still during the above Initial Gyro and Initial Yaw Offset calibration periods. In support of this, the navX MXP indicates when it is calibrating; we recommend you incorporate this information into your software. Please see the discussion of the navXMXP UI, and the navX MXP libraries for more details on this indication.
