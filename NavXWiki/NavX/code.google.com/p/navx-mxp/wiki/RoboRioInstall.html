RoboRioInstall  
Updated Jan 10 (5 days ago) by scottlib...@gmail.com

The navX MXP is designed to be plug-n-play with the National Instruments RoboRio. This installation takes about only a minute.

Installing the navX MXP

To install, simply place the 34-pin "MXP" Connector on the bottom of the navX MXP into the corresponding MXP slot on the top of the RoboRio, as shown below.

Securing the navX MXP to the RoboRio

Next, secure the navX MXP to the RoboRio using two #4-40 screws, each with a length of 3/16th inch. You can also use a 1/4 inch-long screw if you place a small washer between it and the top of the navX MXP MXP board.

Securing the navX MXP and RoboRio to the robot chassis

The navX MXP should be mounted such that it is firmly attached to the robot chassis. The quality of this mounting will be directly reflected in the quality of the inertial measurements provided by the navX MXP. To ensure quality, carefully follow these guidelines:

  • The RoboRio on which the navX is placed should be tightly mounted; it should be a part of the chassis mass, and should move exactly as the chassis moves. Avoid mounting the navX MXP in an area of the chassis that might be flexible, as this could introduce vibration to the inertial sensors that does not represent the chassis inertial properties.
  • The navX MXP should be mounted in the center of the chassis, which ensures the origin of the yaw/pitch/roll axes truly represent the chassis center.
  • Be sure to understand the orientation of the navX MXP, relative to the chassis.
  • Housing the navX MXP in some form of protective enclosure is highly recommended, to protect it from damage. This should both protect the circuit board from damage, and provide strain relief for the cables that connect to the navX MXP.

(Note that there are several other installation options available.)
